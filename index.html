Case study

Tong Farm, Brenchley

Marle Place Road, Tonbridge, Kent TN12 7HS Development High Weald AONB

The project

Tong Farm is a former agricultural site on Marle Place Road in Brenchley, set within the High Weald Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty. The site comprised a range of existing agricultural buildings on a generous plot, presenting an opportunity for a sensitive mixed-use development that responds to the rural character of the surrounding landscape.

The location is well-connected, sitting close to the villages of Brenchley and Horsmonden, with the A21 providing easy access to Tonbridge, Tunbridge Wells, and the wider Kent motorway network.

Planning & approvals

Full planning permission was granted under application reference 22/02640/FULL for the demolition of the existing agricultural buildings and the erection of four dwellings, one self-build home, one live/work unit, and two office buildings — all within the AONB designation. A subsequent variation application (24/03204/FULL) was submitted to refine elements of the approved scheme.

Achieving consent in the High Weald AONB required a design-led approach that demonstrated the development would conserve and enhance the natural beauty of the landscape. Materials were chosen to reflect the local vernacular — black timber cladding, handmade brick, and clay tile roofing — ensuring the scheme sits comfortably within its rural context.

Design approach

The scheme draws on the agricultural heritage of the site, with building forms inspired by traditional Kentish barns and oast houses. Each dwelling has its own distinct character while maintaining a cohesive palette of materials that ties the development together. The layout follows the natural contours of the land, with generous spacing between units to preserve views and maintain the sense of openness that defines the AONB setting.

A mixed-use element — the live/work unit and two office buildings — adds variety and supports the rural economy, in line with local planning policy. The self-build plot offers a buyer the opportunity to create a bespoke home within an already consented framework.

What we delivered

Building Design Studio handled the full design and planning process, from initial feasibility and pre-application discussions through to the detailed drawings submitted for approval. Our role encompassed site analysis, concept design, design development, planning strategy, and coordination with landscape and ecology consultants to address the AONB requirements.
